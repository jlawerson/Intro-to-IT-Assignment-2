Our Opinion

Aaron

Not for me, I enjoy the idea of a challenge, and learning as much as I can about what I am passionate about. The number of areas and fields I need to know to be a successful Full-Stack developer is definitely intimidating, but as with many things, the knowledge and expertise will come with time and experience. I don’t expect to become a Full-Stack developer in a few years, rather it would be a build up over perhaps 5-10 years, working in various jobs. I want to rise to the challenge, and be a valuable asset to any IT development company I may work with.

Alina

After having a look through the burning glass data, my ideal job as being a Web Developer has not changed. Even though there is quite a few skills sets that I need to develop before becoming a Web Developer - I still like the idea that I will be able to put my creativity to good use within this career pathway. Also in the future I could also expand to other areas, for example, mobile applications etc.

Joshua

Having read the burning glass data, my idea of an ideal job has not changed. However, my desire to spread out my skill set has definitely increased. I still want to work in a field that requires constant learning and updating of resources and that sense of competition that would come from working in the security field. However, seeing the demand for certain skills that would originally have been outside this skill set, has made me feel inclined to diversify. Simply to increase my hireability.
