Working as a Team:

As a team, we include two INTJs and an ISFP. Despite INTJs being only 2% of the general population, they are massively over-represented in the IT field.
This makes it likely that at some point in our future we will again be working with other INTJs, meaning that this is a great opportunity to develop our skills in that area.
Our ISFP member, Alina is a great representative of her personality type. ISFPs tend to be charming and more aware of other people’s feelings, making here a great moderator between the two INTJ members of the group. She is also artistic by nature, despite no formal studying in the area, so her work on our website template looks amazing.
Our team contains two tactile learners and an auditory learner, meaning that as a group we will likely be experimenting as we go, but making sure to take the time to discuss it. Overall we have a very creative team, expressing curiosity across the board.

Ideal Job Comparison:

• Aaron: Full-Stack Developer
• Alina: Website Developer
• Josh: Senior/Managing Penetration Tester

Our ideal jobs represent a wide variety within the IT field. A common element within all three fields would be the need to learn at least the one scripting language, if not more, like JavaScript or HTML5.
We will also all require an understanding of at least the one OS, and likely more with Aaron and Josh. Aaron’s ideal role, as a Full-Stack Developer requires an extensive knowledge of different programming languages and also skills within the business world.
This is similar to Josh’s ideal role, as Managing Penetration Tester. Both will require the ability to work well as a part of a team and great communication skills. Time management and of course the Microsoft Office suite will likely be needed by all three of us.
Alina’s ideal role as a Website developer will likely allow her to have a more creative input into her work, letting her express that part of her personality. That’s not to say that all three roles aren’t creative, as they all involve complex problem solving, a deeply creative task.
